01 — Overview
The Assignment
We need a small library of usable assets from every event night — not a gallery of 300 mediocre shots. Quality over volume. We want images that look like Stardust, not like every other bar's Instagram.
Goal: 20–30 edited selects per event, delivered within 48 hours. Every image should be usable without a caption — the photo tells the story on its own.
02 — Shot List
What We Need
Hero Performer Shots
  • Singer at the mic, lit well, mid-song
  • Capture the face — emotion over technical perfection
  • The hesitation before a singer commits, then the moment they do
Crowd Energy
  • Wide shots showing the room alive — density, raised drinks, people watching the stage
  • The communal singalong moment — multiple people, not just the performer
  • The crowd joining in: arms around each other, everyone knowing the words
Candid Moments
  • Laughter between songs, friends reacting
  • The bartender during a peak moment
  • The payoff after a performer finishes — applause, cheers, crowd reaction
Detail & Atmosphere
  • The logo, glassware in warm light, the booth, the lights doing something interesting
  • The stage empty and lit — mic stand in the wash, warm light, anticipation
  • One or two "clean" vertical shots per night suitable for Stories / TikTok covers

03 — Technical
Technical Guidelines
Format
Shoot RAW if possible; JPG fine if turnaround is fast
Flash
No harsh on-camera flash — use the stage and practical lighting. Flash kills the vibe and the shot.
Aperture
Wide aperture preferred: f/1.8–f/2.8 for shallow depth and low-light performance
Color Grade
Grade warm: lift shadows slightly, keep blacks deep, pull highlights back. Avoid cold blue tones.
Vertical Crop
Shoot or crop at least 2 portrait-oriented shots per night at 1080×1920px (9:16) for Stories and TikTok covers
Deliverable
Selects folder of 20–30 edited images, delivered to the Stardust Google Drive within 48 hours of the event
Color palette reference: Deep midnight backgrounds · Stardust Gold (#d4a84a) warmth · Soft purple (#9b8fd4) mystery. Photography should lean warm and slightly desaturated — practical lighting from candles, neon, and stage wash.
04 — Style
Do & Don't
Do
  • Candid performer moments over posed shots
  • Warm practical lighting — candles, neon, stage wash
  • Crowd energy: wide shots that show the room alive
  • Shoot the moment before someone sings and after — the arc
  • Make the room look packed — wait for the right moment
  • Tight framing if the crowd is thin — 20 people can look like 60
Don't
  • Over-edited "HDR nightclub" look — keep it natural and warm
  • Posed group shots (unless requested by patrons)
  • Blurry crowd shots where no one is recognizable
  • Anything that makes the room look empty — wait for the right moment
  • Flash photography during performances
  • Cold blue color grading

05 — Night Of
Night-Of Schedule
The lighting arc matters — your shot opportunities change throughout the night. Plan accordingly.
6:00–8:00pm
Happy Hour / Warm-up — Doors open, happy hour running, minimal static lighting. Lower energy, intimate bar atmosphere. Good for: detail shots, early arrivals, room-establishing wides before the crowd fills in.
8:00–10:00pm
Build — Happy hour ends, light movement introduced, deeper blues and golds coming in. The room is filling and alive. Good for: performer hero shots, crowd energy, the singalong moment.
10:00pm+
Full tilt — Moving bars active, star lights on, full color palette. Good for: peak energy crowd shots, performers at their most committed, the room at its best.
Close
Last call — Friday closes at 4am, Saturday at 5am. Late-night shots: the crowd that stayed, the last singer, the room at its most honest.
If the room is thin: Don't adjust your approach — the sound and the show continue regardless. Use tight framing to capture energy and intimacy. A small crowd that's genuinely having a night beats an empty-looking wide shot.
Electro Swing nights specifically: The Electro Swing launch features staged props — a feathered accessory, white gloves, a 1920s hat, sheet music from the 1940s placed on the bar. These are intentional visual elements. Shoot them warm, close, and mysterious. Art deco meets modern.
